Who is John Albert Elway: Biography, Net Worth, Career, &amp; more

John Albert Elway Jr., an American professional football executive, and former quarterback were born on June 28, 1960. He currently serves as the president of football operations for the National Football League’s Denver Broncos (NFL).

Elway spent his entire 16-year professional career with the Denver Broncos while playing collegiate football for the Stanford Cardinal. He is frequently considered one of the greatest quarterbacks in history. Elway has the most victories of any starting quarterback at the time of his retirement in early 1999, and he was statistically the second-most effective passer in NFL history. He was one of just two players—the other being Thurman Thomas—and the only quarterback to score a running touchdown in four different Super Bowls. He was also a productive runner of the ball. In addition, Elway is just one of two quarterbacks (the other being Peyton Manning, who played for the Denver Broncos) to leave the game after winning the Super Bowl.

While at Stanford, Elway set numerous records for passing attempts and completions and was also named All-American. He was selected by the Baltimore Colts as the first pick in the 1983 NFL Draft, which is infamously known as the quarterback class of 1983, before being traded to the Denver Broncos. In the AFC Championship Game against the Cleveland Browns in January 1987, Elway participated in one of the most illustrious performances in sports and NFL history. This drive, which covered 98 yards and tied the game with a touchdown, became known as “The Drive.” Elway and the Broncos were defeated by the New York Giants in the Super Bowl XXI after that defeat in Cleveland.

The Broncos went through a period of decline following two consecutive Super Bowl defeats, but that was put to an end during the 1997 campaign when Elway and Denver earned their first Super Bowl championship by defeating the Green Bay Packers 31-24 in Super Bowl XXXII. The Denver Broncos defeated the Atlanta Falcons 34-19 in Super Bowl XXXIII to capture the title again the next year. Elway started five Super Bowls, which was a record at the time that was broken in February 2015 when Tom Brady of the New England Patriots started Super Bowl XLIX. Elway was named MVP of the Super Bowl, which was also his final game. Elway worked for the Broncos as general manager and executive vice president of football operations following his retirement as a player. During his time there, the team won four division championships, two AFC Championships, and Super Bowl 50. This made Elway a three-time Super Bowl champion with the team—two as a player and one as an executive. Elway was admitted to both the Pro Football Hall of Fame and the College Football Hall of Fame in 2000 and 2004, respectively.
