Anastasia Potapova is a famous Russian professional tennis player who was born on March 30, 2001, in Saratov, Russia.

She started playing tennis at the age of six and turned professional in 2016.

Potapova has had a successful junior career, winning the girls’ singles title at the 2016 Australian Open and reaching the final of the girls’ singles event at Wimbledon in the same year. She also won the girls’ doubles title at Wimbledon in 2016.

In her professional career, Potapova has won two WTA singles titles. Her first title came at the age of 17 when she won the Moscow River Cup in July 2018.

She defeated fellow Russian player, Natalia Vikhlyantseva, in straight sets to win her maiden title. Her second title came at the Istanbul Cup in April 2021, where she defeated Romanian player Sorana Cirstea in three sets.

Potapova’s highest ranking on the WTA Tour is world No. 64, which she achieved in February 2020.

She has also represented Russia in Fed Cup matches and helped her country reach the final of the competition in 2019.

Potapova is known for her aggressive playing style and powerful groundstrokes. She has been compared to former world No.1 Maria Sharapova due to their similar playing styles and Russian heritage.

At just 20 years old, Anastasia Potapova has already achieved a lot in her tennis career and looks set to continue making waves on the WTA Tour for years to come.
